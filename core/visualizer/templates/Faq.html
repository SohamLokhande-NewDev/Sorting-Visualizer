Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about SortViz.

General
SortViz is an interactive sorting algorithm visualiser that uses image slices. Upload any image, choose the number of slices, and watch Bubble, Insertion, Quick, and Merge Sort compete to reconstruct it in the correct order.
No. You can upload images and run all visualisations without logging in. An account lets you save your history and download reports.
Uploaded images and their slices are stored temporarily on the server. They are associated with a unique image ID and may be cleared periodically. Do not upload sensitive or private images.
Visualiser
The backend pre-computes every swap and comparison step for each algorithm. Each "frame" is one step in the sorting process. The frontend plays through these frames sequentially, updating the displayed slice order at each step.
Sky blue highlights indicate slices being compared. Orange highlights indicate slices actively being swapped. This helps you see what the algorithm is doing at each step.
Yes. Use the Speed slider in the top control bar. Moving it right increases speed (fewer milliseconds per frame). Moving it left slows the animation down for detailed observation.
8–16 slices is a good balance for visibility and interesting animations. Fewer slices (4) complete quickly. More slices (32) produce more dramatic difference between O(n²) and O(n log n) algorithms.
Algorithms
Bubble Sort has O(n²) complexity. For n slices, it may require up to n×(n-1)/2 comparisons. With 16 slices that can be up to 120 steps, compared to ~48 for merge sort.
Merge Sort performs many moves during the merge step, creating many frames even though its total count is lower. Each frame represents one logical operation, not one visual swap.
Quick Sort and Merge Sort generally win on frame count. However results depend on the initial shuffle. The Report page highlights the winner for your specific run. The Comparison page lets you race all four simultaneously.
Reports & PDF
Navigate to the Report page from the Visualiser. Click "Download PDF" — this calls the backend endpoint which generates a formatted PDF with all algorithm stats, charts, and observations.
The PDF includes a summary table comparing all four algorithms (frames, complexity), a bar chart of performance, the best algorithm highlighted, and written observations about the run.
